## üéØ Overview: What is a Minor at UCalgary Engineering

* Minors are **optional**, additional programs you can combine with your engineering major. ([Schulich School of Engineering][1])
* Students typically apply for a minor in their **first year (or early second year)**. ([University of Calgary Calendar][2])
* To get the minor ‚Äúon your transcript,‚Äù you must complete the required courses for the minor in addition to your major‚Äôs requirements. ([University of Calgary in Alberta][3])
* Because engineering programs are already demanding, minors add extra workload. In many cases, you need to plan carefully to avoid overloading yourself.

## üìò Breakdown: What Each Minor Entails

### **Aerospace Engineering**

* **Purpose / Focus:** Design and development of aircraft/spacecraft ‚Äî avionics, propulsion, drone/remote systems, navigation, sensors. ([Schulich School of Engineering][5])
* **Who can take it:** Electrical, Geomatics, Mechanical, or Software engineering students (from 2nd year onward) ([Schulich School of Engineering][5])
* **What you study:** Aerospace-specific engineering courses (aircraft/vehicle design, control systems, remote sensing/avionics, propulsion, structural/flight dynamics). ([Schulich School of Engineering][5])
* **Technical Electives:** The minor allows approved technical electives (streams vary: e.g. ‚ÄúAerodynamics & Propulsion‚Äù, ‚ÄúAutonomous Systems ‚Äì Sensing & Control‚Äù, or ‚ÄúAutonomous Mapping & Navigation‚Äù). Approved electives come from departments like Mechanical, Electrical/Software, Geomatics. ([University of Calgary in Alberta][11])
* **Considerations:** Useful if you're interested in aerospace industry, drones/ UAVs, or satellite/spacecraft design. Could add considerable workload depending on electives chosen.

### **Biomedical Engineering (BME)**

* **Purpose / Focus:** Combines engineering and life sciences: biomechanics, biomaterials, tissue engineering, medical devices, imaging, bioprocessing, etc. ([Schulich School of Engineering][6])
* **Who can take it:** Chemical, Civil, Electrical, Geomatics, Mechanical, Software engineering students (from 2nd year) ([Schulich School of Engineering][6])
* **What you study:** Foundational courses in biomedical engineering + electives in areas like biomedical devices, biomaterials, bio-processing, biomedical signal processing, etc. ([Schulich School of Engineering][6])
* **Why consider it:** Great for students interested in medical devices, healthcare, biotechnology, or bridging engineering with life sciences. Good preparation for grad school, medical school, or biomedical-related industry. ([Schulich School of Engineering][6])

### **Digital Engineering**

* **Purpose / Focus:** Gain computing, software, AI/ML, IoT, and systems-level digital skills useful across all fields. ([Schulich School of Engineering][7])
* **Who can take it:** Chemical, Civil, Electrical, Geomatics, Mechanical, Biomedical*, Sustainable Systems among others (from 2nd year) ([Schulich School of Engineering][7])
* **What you study:** Programming, software engineering fundamentals, data/algorithm courses, digital systems, emerging digital technologies. ([Schulich School of Engineering][7])
* **Why consider it:** Digital skills are increasingly valuable across engineering disciplines (automation, control systems, data analysis, embedded systems). Adds flexibility and broadens career options ‚Äî especially if your major is hardware-heavy but you want software/digital exposure.

### **Mechatronics Engineering**

* **Purpose / Focus:** Blend of mechanical, electrical, and software ‚Äî robotics, embedded systems, smart machines, control systems. ([Schulich School of Engineering][8])
* **Who can take it:** Mechanical, Electrical, or Software engineering students (from 2nd year) ([Schulich School of Engineering][8])
* **What you study:** Courses across mechanical design, electronics, control theory, embedded systems, and software controlling physical systems. ([Schulich School of Engineering][8])
* **Why consider it:** Excellent for students interested in robotics, automation, intelligent systems, or cross-disciplinary design. Combines strengths of multiple engineering domains.

### **Energy and Environment Engineering**

* **Purpose / Focus:** Understanding energy systems **and** their environmental impacts ‚Äî sustainable energy, mitigation, resource management, environmental engineering aspects. ([University of Calgary Calendar][4])
* **Who can take it:** Chemical, Civil, Electrical, Geomatics, Mechanical engineering students (from 2nd year) ([University of Calgary Calendar][4])
* **Use cases:** Suitable for students interested in sustainability, renewable energy, environmental consulting, resource/energy industries.

### **Entrepreneurship and Enterprise Development (MEED)**

* **Purpose / Focus:** Business / entrepreneurship training ‚Äî valuable for engineers interested in startups, business management, venture creation, tech commercialization. ([University of Calgary in Alberta][3])
* **Who can take it:** All engineering majors (typically starting 2nd year) ([University of Calgary in Alberta][3])
* **What you study:** Five courses in entrepreneurship/innovation/business management (over and above engineering degree requirements). Courses may include ‚ÄúEntrepreneurship and Innovation 201‚Äù, ‚ÄúENTI 381‚Äù, ‚ÄúENTI 401‚Äù, ‚ÄúENTI 405‚Äù, plus one business elective (e.g. marketing, finance, operations management). ([University of Calgary in Alberta][3])
* **Requirements:** You need to receive credit for an extra 15 units beyond your engineering degree. ([University of Calgary in Alberta][3])
* **Why consider it:** Useful if you want to start your own company, lead projects, or combine engineering with business/management roles.

### Other Minors / Concentrations (More Specialized / Less Common)

* **Manufacturing Engineering (minor):** Available to Mechanical Engineering students. Focus on sustainable manufacturing, production systems, and related skills. ([Schulich School of Engineering][9])
* **Petroleum Engineering (minor):** Possible for some majors (e.g. Mechanical) or via transfer from Chemical. Geared toward oil & gas industry, reservoir engineering, resource extraction. ([University of Calgary Calendar][4])
* **Structural Engineering (minor/concentration):** For Civil Engineering students ‚Äî design and analysis of structures (buildings, bridges, infrastructure). ([University of Calgary Calendar][4])
* **Transportation Engineering (minor/concentration):** Civil Engineering students focusing on transportation systems, planning, traffic engineering, highways, transit systems. ([University of Calgary Calendar][4])
* **Computer Engineering (minor):** For Electrical Engineering students ‚Äî covers fundamentals of computing, circuits, embedded systems, etc. ([Schulich School of Engineering][10])

## ‚úÖ Key Considerations When Choosing a Minor

* **Workload is heavier:** Minors add courses beyond your major‚Äôs requirements. Some (like MEED) demand 15 extra units. ([University of Calgary in Alberta][3])
* **Application timing matters:** Most minors require you to apply early (first or second year). For certain minors (e.g. those tied to 3rd/4th-year coursework), you may need to be ‚Äúon track‚Äù in your major. ([University of Calgary Calendar][2])
* **Not all majors can take all minors:** Always check the allowable combinations table. ([University of Calgary Calendar][4])
* **Electives / Technical Electives vs fixed minor courses:** Many minors require approved technical electives ‚Äî you often have some freedom to choose from a list, as long as prerequisites are satisfied. ([University of Calgary in Alberta][11])
* **Planning ahead is essential:** Because engineering programs are tightly structured, adding a minor may require extra semesters or careful scheduling to avoid overload (especially if you want to do internships).
* **Minor ‚â† automatic specialization:** Having a minor shows interest/competency in an area, but in many industries, **experience (internships, projects, clubs) matters just as much or more** than the minor itself. Several students report that a minor alone doesn‚Äôt guarantee a job in the specialty. ([Reddit][12])

## üéì How to Decide ‚Äî What to Ask Yourself

Before picking a minor, consider:

* **What kind of career or industries interest you?** If you want aerospace / drones ‚Üí Aerospace minor. Robotics/automation ‚Üí Mechatronics. Renewable energy ‚Üí Energy & Environment, etc.
* **Willingness to take more courses / longer degree time?** Minors add workload.
* **Desire for breadth vs depth:** Do you want extra skills (e.g. software + engineering) or deeper specialization?
* **Complementing your major:** Some minors pair naturally with certain majors (e.g. Electrical + Digital/Mechatronics, Mechanical + Aerospace/Mechatronics, Civil + Structural/Transportation).
* **Long-term goals:** For entrepreneurship, management, or interdisciplinary work ‚Äî MEED or Digital may add value. For technical specialization ‚Äî minors like Petroleum, Energy & Environment, or Mechatronics may help.

# üìö Minor Requirements at UCalgary Engineering ‚Äî Detailed Guide

## üìù General rules for ALL minors

* For a minor you must complete **at least 30 units** in the minor field, of which **at least 18 units must be 300-level or above**. ([University of Calgary in Alberta][1])
* GPA requirements: Minimum 2.00 GPA over all courses counting toward the minor; at most 3 units with a ‚ÄúD‚Äù or ‚ÄúD+‚Äù grade are allowed. ([University of Calgary in Alberta][1])
* There can be overlap between your major and minor course requirements, but the amount depends on the combination; you must check with the Associate Dean (Undergraduate) if there‚Äôs considerable overlap. ([University of Calgary in Alberta][1])
* To apply for a minor: you apply in first year (for most minors). If demand exceeds capacity, admission to the minor is competitive, based on your most recent 10 courses. ([University of Calgary Calendar][2])

**Available for**: Electrical (ENEL), Geomatics (ENGO), Mechanical (ENME), Software (ENSF) ‚Äî from 2nd year. ([Schulich School of Engineering][3])

**Core required courses:** ([University of Calgary in Alberta][4])

**Why / When to pick it:** Good if you want skills in aircraft/spacecraft design, avionics, drones, autonomous aerial or remote-sensing systems ‚Äî especially effective if your main major is mechanical, electrical, geomatics or software. ([Schulich School of Engineering][3])

## ‚úÖ What to Watch Out For / Important Conditions

* **Application process & timing:** You must apply for a minor (usually in first year); admission is *not* guaranteed and is based on academic performance (recent courses). ([University of Calgary Calendar][2])
* **Prerequisites and scheduling constraints:** Some minor courses have prerequisites (especially in Aero, Mechatronics, BME) that may conflict with major course scheduling ‚Äî requires careful planning. ([University of Calgary in Alberta][4])
* **Overlap and credit limits:** Overlap between major and minor may be limited; you may not be allowed to ‚Äúdouble-count‚Äù certain courses. ([University of Calgary in Alberta][1])
* **Workload:** A minor can add a nontrivial amount of courses (‚â• 30 units), which increases your total course load. For heavy majors (Mechanical, Electrical, Chemical, etc.), minor + major + work-term/internship may push you beyond a standard 4-year path (or make your semesters very heavy).
* **Electives list changes:** The list of approved technical electives for minors gets updated periodically. Always refer to the most recent calendar‚Äôs ‚ÄúApproved Technical Electives for Minors.‚Äù ([University of Calgary in Alberta][8])
* **Administrative approval needed:** If there‚Äôs significant overlap between the major and minor requirements (e.g. shared technical electives), you must discuss and get approval from the Associate Dean (Undergraduate). ([University of Calgary in Alberta][1])
